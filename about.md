## about this site

lakṣaṇa-pramāṇābhyām vastu-siddhiḥ -- to know a vastu is 
to understand the lakṣaṇa and pramāṇas for it.

Traditional study starts with the tarka saṅgraha, artha saṅgraha, 
vedānta paribhāṣā and siddhānta leśa saṅgraha. 
They focus on both learning and retention -- grahaṇa-dhāraṇa-paṭuḥ bālaḥ.

The gr̥hastha audience typically studies in their own mother tongue, 
or in English, and avoids the lakṣaṇa vākyas. For those that have basic
fluency with samskr̥t, the resources on this site may be useful. 

It provides a graded approach to vedānta, rooted in lakṣaṇas.

1. tattva bodha -- the shortest samskr̥ta primer on vedānta
2. vicāra candrodaya -- an intermediate primer, with more precise lakṣaṇas
3. vicāra sāgara -- a comprehensive primer, covering all major prakriyās
4. upaniṣads -- with the simple prasāda and maṇiprabhā commentaries
