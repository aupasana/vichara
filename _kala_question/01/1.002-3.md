<div class="skt" markdown="1">
सकलजनानां दुःखनिवृत्तौ सुखावाप्तौ च ईदृशी इच्छा जायते।
मोक्षः सर्वदुःखनिवृत्तिः परमानन्दप्राप्तिश्च। स एव परमं प्रयोजनम्। 
</div>

<div class="translation" markdown="1">
All people desire freedom from sorrow, and the attainment of happiness.
Freedom from sorrow, and the attainment of happiness is called mokṣa (liberation). 
</div>

<div class="footnote skt" markdown="1">
अज्ञानसहितं जन्ममरणादिकं दुःखम्। तस्य निवृत्तिः बाधरूपा मिथ्यात्मकनिश्चयभूता। (कल्पितनामरूपात्मकसकलवस्तुनिवृत्तिरपि तदधिष्ठानचिन्मात्रमेव, न ततोऽतिरिक्ता इति सिद्धम्। तदुक्तं वार्तिके “अधिष्ठानावशेषो हि नाशः कल्पितवस्तुनः” इति)
</div>

<div class="footnote-eng translation" markdown="1">
Sorrow is ajñāna (the cause) along with  (it's effects) such as birth and death. 
The removal (nivr̥tti) of ajñāna is the conviction of it’s falsity (mithyātva). Removing that which is kalpita (superimposed) is nothing other than it’s adhiṣṭhāna’s avaśeṣa -- the substratum which alone is true.
</div>

<div class="skt" markdown="1">
परमानन्दः परमप्रेमास्पदीभूतविषयः। तस्य नित्यप्राप्तस्य प्राप्तिः स्वकण्ठाभरणं यथा। कर्तृत्वभोक्तृत्वादिरहितं स्वस्वरूपावस्थानं मोक्षः।
</div>

<div class="translation" markdown="1">
Happiness is the object of the greatest affection. 
Like a necklace which has gone out of sight around one’s own neck,
one obtains only that which is already obtained.
Mokṣa is abiding in one’s own svarūpa, devoid of thoughts of doership, enjoyership etc.
</div>

<div class="skt" markdown="1">
केचन वैकुण्ठब्रह्मलोकादिप्राप्तिं एव मोक्षं मन्यन्ते। किञ्च तद्वेदविरुद्धम्॥
</div>

<div class="translation" markdown="1">
Some think that mokṣa is reaching higher lokas such as vaikuṇṭha and brahma loka. 
But, this is opposed to the teachings of the vedas.
</div>
